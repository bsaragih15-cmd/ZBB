# MPI Cost Cockpit — voiceover script (ElevenLabs)

Timed to the ~52s guided tour (`demo-videos/zbb-cockpit-tour-1080.mp4`).
~140 words ≈ 50–52s at a calm, confident pace.

## Suggested ElevenLabs settings
- **Voice:** a calm, professional narrator (e.g. "Adam", "Daniel", or a corporate male/female VO).
- **Model:** Eleven Multilingual v2 (or Turbo v2.5 for speed).
- **Stability** ~45–55 · **Similarity** ~75 · **Style** ~0–15 · Speaker boost on.

## Pronunciation guide (say it this way)
- **MRPR** → “em-are-pee-are”  · **ELB** → “ee-el-bee” · **MEB+DEB** → “em-ee-bee plus dee-ee-bee”
- **O&M** → “oh and em”
- **$/kW-yr** → “dollars per kilowatt-year”
- **L3 / L4 / L5** → “level three / level four / level five”
- **Rp** (Rupiah) → just read the number, e.g. “eighteen billion rupiah”

## Plain script (paste this into ElevenLabs)

This is the MPI Cost Cockpit — a zero-based budgeting tool that stress-tests an entire fleet of power plants.

It benchmarks every plant's controllable oh-and-em against the best in the fleet. Two of three run above em-are-pee-are's forty-five dollars per kilowatt-year.

Set the ambition, and the cost-out updates live — capture any share of the gap you choose.

Drill into any plant. At level three and four, every cost block is decomposed and benchmarked, then expanded to its driver families.

And all the way down to level five — every line as quantity, times frequency, times unit rate, times exchange rate, each with its own budget code. This is the granularity you'll budget to.

Ask the built-in Copilot. It reasons over the real numbers and hands you the exact challenge questions for each budget owner.
